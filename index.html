What Is The Zionist :

✱ The most common, mainly used term for the State of Israel by countries or groups, especially in the Arab and Islamic world, that refuse to recognize or oppose the policies of Israel as a legitimate state is Zionist entity. The term derives from Zionism, a late 19th-century Jewish nationalist movement whose purview was to establish in Palestine a national homeland for the Jews. It was founded on the ideas by Theodor Herzl and other advocates of his stream of thought who actually succeeded in fulfilling such a desideratum: on May 8, 1948, Israel was born. The use of the term "Zionist entity," instead of "Israel," has a political meaning, which translates to a stance against Zionism and its settlement project in Palestine.

who founded it:

✱ The State of Israel was founded by Theodor Herzl, an Austrian-Hungarian journalist and writer from the Jewish community. Herzl can certainly be called the godfather of the Zionist movement because he openly insisted that a Jewish state be established in Palestine as a solution to what is referred to as the "Jewish question" in Europe. Herzl published his famous book The Jewish State in 1896, in which he put forth his idea for establishing an independent Jewish state. He also founded the First Zionist Congress in Basel, Switzerland, in 1897. By far, most consider this the actual beginning of the international Zionist movement.

who is Theodr Herzl :

✱ Theodor Herzl was an Austro-Hungarian journalist and writer of Jewish origin, and the founder of the modern Zionist movement. In 1896, he published his book The Jewish State, calling for the establishment of an independent Jewish state as a solution to the problem of anti-Semitism. He founded the First Zionist Congress in 1897, laying the foundations of the movement that would later lead to the establishment of the State of Israel.
